Reading the story

Your village keeps running whether you're watching or not. The chronicle is how you catch up on everything that happened while you were away.

The chronicle: your village's own storyteller

Because the world never pauses on its own, promptworld needs a way to hand you a readable summary when you come back after a day, a week, or longer. That's the chronicle: a cloud AI model periodically reads through everything that happened and writes it up as a few short story entries — a chapter or two per game day, not a blow-by-blow transcript. It covers things like: deaths, buildings going up, sightings and attacks by the "gru" (a nocturnal threat that stalks the village at night), conversations (with a gist of what was discussed), rumors spreading, gifts given, promises broken, thefts from a chest, passing musings, and the village's own self-governance — meetings, grievances aired, proposals voted up or down, and any exiles. Each entry is stamped with the in-world time it covers, so the story reads in order.

A day or night that has nothing notable in it costs nothing and gets no filler entry — you only get chapters when something actually happened.

The chronicle travels with your world's save data, so the moment you reattach — no matter how long you were gone — the recent story is already there waiting, with no separate step needed to fetch it. If the narrator ever has a hiccup, the unwritten lines simply carry over to the next chapter instead of getting lost, and if the village has no AI configured at all, you just don't get narrated chapters — the raw event feed described below still works.

Where to read it

  • The full-screen client's Chronicle pane — the default tab, right there when you open promptworld ui demo. It shows the narrated story with filters by villager and by storyline thread. Press r to flip to the raw event-by-event feed instead (this is also the automatic fallback if no narrated entries exist yet); press it again to flip back.
  • The command line — promptworld tail demo --follow streams the raw event feed live in your terminal (drop --follow to just see recent history, which works even with the world not currently running). promptworld attach demo gives you the same kind of live raw feed alongside simple typed commands.
  • A plain text file — chronicle.md, sitting right in the world's save directory, regenerated automatically. You can open it in any text editor, no game required.

Reading the raw feed

When you're looking at the raw feed instead of the narrated chronicle, each line reads as a short, plain-language summary of one thing that happened — for example "Ash foraged at (14,9)" or a line of dialogue shown as "Ash→Rowan: ...". A handful of especially important moments (a death, an attack, a chest theft, a village-law violation) are visually called out so they don't slip by in a busy feed. If your villagers are AI-driven, you'll also occasionally see a governor shed or governor recovered line — that's the world automatically dialing its own speed down a notch to keep villagers' thinking sensible, then dialing back up once it catches up; see the time & speed page for the full story. In the full-screen client, pausing the world lets you select any entry and see its full raw detail underneath.

Nothing is ever rewritten

Under all of this sits one append-only history of every single thing that has ever happened in your world — nothing in it can be edited or deleted after the fact. The chronicle and the raw feed are both just readable windows onto that same permanent record, so what you're reading is always the real history, not a reconstruction.
