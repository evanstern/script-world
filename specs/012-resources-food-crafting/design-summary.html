Resources, Food & Crafting v1

How the village economy works after v1: three gatherable resources, a fine-grained food currency with cooking, and a five-item crafting layer with two intermediate materials — all on the deterministic executor, with survival still guaranteed when no mind is running.

The economy at a glance

Everything flows one way: terrain is gathered into raw resources, raw resources are refined into intermediates or cooked into better food, and intermediates become the items and stations that improve gathering and cooking. Fuel is the loop-closer — fire and oven both burn wood, so wood never stops mattering.

The resource layer

Three base resources, each with its own source, action, and character.

Wood

chop adjacent tree · exists today

The universal material — and now also the universal fuel. Beyond building, wood is burned by fires (to stay lit) and by ovens (per batch), so demand never plateaus. Chopped trees clear the tile, as today.

Stone

quarry adjacent rock outcrop · new

A new rock-outcrop terrain kind, noise-placed in coherent patches on ~6% of dry land. Quarrying works like chopping: stand adjacent, work, take the yield; the outcrop depletes permanently. Stone is finite but tuned to be practically inexhaustible — and survival never needs it.

This is a format-versioned map change: every seed regenerates with outcrops.

Water

collect beside any water tile · new

An ingredient, not a need — there is no thirst in v1, no container, and no death by dehydration. Water's only consumer is the oven bath; it exists now so future recipes (stews, mortar, whatever the village invents) have a supply chain waiting.

The food system

Food stops being "meals" and becomes a fine-grained unit: a berry is 1, a rabbit is 8. What changes the game is the raw/cooked split — the same gathered unit is worth up to 2.5× more after passing through the village's infrastructure.

Eating is one action that consumes units — most nutritious first — until the villager is sated (food need ≥ 900 of 1000) or the pouch is empty. The need scale and hunger thresholds are unchanged; only the currency got finer. For reference, today's system was one indivisible +350 meal.

The survival floor holds. A village with no planner at all still lives: forage and hunt bare-handed, eat raw, chop wood, build and refuel fires. That raw loop is the entire reflex vocabulary — verified by the spec's success criterion that a planner-less village survives 3+ game days with zero crafting events.

The five items

Each item earned its own discussion in the session; these are the settled mechanics.

Fire

build on site · 2 wood → 8h of burn

  • Fuel: burns 4 game-hours per wood; refuel with 1 wood, capped at 12 hours remaining.
  • Burnout: an untended fire goes cold — no warmth, no cooking. It persists as a structure and relights on refuel.
  • Cooks: raw → cooked (+80) for anyone beside it while lit.
  • The one item the reflex still manages: build when cold nights demand, refuel when carrying wood.

Basic Shelter

build on site · 8 planks

  • Re-costed from raw wood into planks — the wood chain's first load-bearing customer.
  • Warmth exactly as today, plus faster rest recovery when sleeping there.
  • Communal: no ownership in v1. Who owns what is TASK-26 territory.

Oven

build on site · 4 refined stone + 2 planks

  • The flagship station — the first thing in the world that needs the stone chain.
  • Fuel from day one: every batch action burns 1 wood; no wood, no effect.
  • Cook: up to 8 raw units → meals (+100 each) per batch.
  • Bathe: 1 water + fuel → +150 morale, +300 warmth (capped). Water's only v1 consumer — and prime chronicle material.
  • Cooking needs no water in v1; water recipes are the intended future.

Spear

hand-craft anywhere · 1 wood + 1 refined stone

  • The first carried tool. Hunting still works bare-handed (8 raw, full duration) — no tool gate on survival.
  • With a spear: 12 raw units at two-thirds the time.
  • Durability 3: each hunt spends a use; the last one breaks it, the villager remembers, and the re-craft loop begins.

Food (the placeholder)

forage & hunt yields, in three forms

  • All forage/hunt output is abstract food units — no per-species items in v1 (a rabbit is 8 units, not a rabbit).
  • The three forms (raw / cooked / meal) are the whole food item system until distinct ingredients arrive in a later version.

Who does what

The execution model and the mind split — the two structural decisions that keep the feature honest.

Hand-craft vs. build-on-site

Portable things — planks, refined stone, spear — are crafted anywhere as timed work intents that re-validate and consume inputs on completion.

Structures — fire, shelter, oven — remain build-on-site intents exactly like today's builds, just with new costs. There is deliberately no craft-then-place mechanic in v1.

Reflex vs. planner

Reflex (degraded mode): forage, hunt bare-handed, eat raw, chop, build + refuel fire. Nothing else — subsistence living.

Planner only: quarry, collect water, all crafting, all cooking, shelter, oven, spear, baths. Every new goal is planner-choosable and works as a guarded plan step. Civilization requires minds.

Scope edges

Deferred to TASK-26 (inventory & storage): carry capacity, stacking, chests, stockpiles, ownership. This spec treats inventory as an abstract "agents hold items" interface — unbounded and lost on death, as today. TASK-26's spec is an input to TASK-50's plan phase.

Compatibility: this is a world-format break. Outcrops change every seed's terrain and the food rescale changes every yield constant, so pre-feature worlds are invalidated — the daemon refuses to load them rather than migrating (worlds are regenerable dev artifacts today). New event types remain no-ops under old replay code; cross-version replay equivalence is explicitly not promised.

All the substrate guarantees hold within the new format: every happening is an event through the reducer, payloads record outcomes (never dice rolls), integer math only, and replaying a log reproduces byte-identical state.
